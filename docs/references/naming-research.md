# Final naming verdict: admedi confirmed — hosted under creational.ai/admedi

**admedi** is the confirmed name for the multi-surface ad mediation platform. After two rounds of research — 17 candidates evaluated total — admedi scores highest across brand strength, semantic clarity, and scalability. It signals "ad mediation" to an adtech audience, works as a Python import and CLI command, and has a clean software namespace. All products and services will live under **creational.ai/admedi** — no separate domain needed.

This research evaluated 17 candidates across two rounds. Round 1 screened 14 names (2 carried forward + 12 fresh): admediate, medio, admedio, medops, admux, tierform, adtier, medibase, adsync, admed, mediateai, admesh, tiersync, medifi, admedly, mediatier. Round 2 verified the top 3 survivors and tested 3 additional candidates (adintel, adflow, mediator). All findings below reflect verified research as of 2026-02-25.

---

## The graveyard: 14 candidates with fatal conflicts

Every name below has at least one disqualifying problem. Understanding *why* they fail narrows the viable namespace considerably.

**admediate** — the most painful elimination. Accedo, a Swedish TV app company, owns **AdMediate®** as a *registered trademark* (note the ®) for a product that is *literally an ad mediation platform for TV apps*. Same product category, same industry, trademarked since 2013. The .com is active. Legal risk is extreme and non-negotiable.

**medio** — triple disqualification. Medio Systems raised **$44.6M** in venture funding before Nokia/HERE acquired them in 2014. They have a Wikipedia article, Bloomberg profile, and Crunchbase listing. Worse, `medio` on PyPI is taken by an active medical imaging library (`from medio import read_img`), making `import medio` permanently unavailable. The .com is held.

**adintel** — the project's working directory name, but fatally compromised in the marketplace. **Nielsen acquired a company literally named "AdIntel" from Ebiquity Plc in 2018 for £26M**, triggering a UK CMA Phase II investigation. Nielsen operates "Ad Intel" as its flagship advertising intelligence product across TV, digital, print, radio, and cinema, with active portals at adintel.global.nielsen.com, adintel.login.nielsen.com, and adintel.uk.nielsen.com. Worse, **ironSource/Unity** — the very API provider this project integrates with — has an internal "AdIntel" product division (confirmed via Crunchbase and job listings). Beyond Nielsen and ironSource, at least 6 additional entities use the name: an Estonian AI startup at ad-intel.com (founded 2020, adintel.ai registered 2025), an AI ad analysis platform at adintel.net, a Salesforce AppExchange app by Success Software, AdIntel Digital Ventures (marketing agency), ADINTEL GROUP LLC (Virginia consultancy), and ADINTEL LTD (UK advertising agency). All domains are taken (.com parked since 2002, .io redirects to another business, .ai is the Estonian startup, .net is active). "Ad intel" is also a generic industry term — GroupM, EssenceMediacom, VaynerMedia, and Ebiquity all use "AdIntel" as a category term for competitive advertising intelligence. SEO is impossible; the University of Chicago Booth School hosts a "Nielsen Ad Intel Dataset" as a major academic research resource. PyPI and npm are available, but the brand and trademark conflicts are disqualifying.

**adflow** — saturated beyond recovery. At least 8-10 active companies use the name: **Adflow B.V.** (Netherlands, e-commerce creative automation SaaS, ~$5.2M revenue, serves Carhartt and O'Neill), **AdFlow Solutions LLC** (publisher monetization platform at adflow.ai), **Fluent Inc.** (NASDAQ: FLNT, launched "adflow" as a commerce media product with an npm SDK), **ADFLOW Networks** (acquired by Daktronics in 2016, holds the github.com/adflow org), **AdFlow Systems GmbH** (Germany, publisher ad management since 2003, serves Funke Mediengruppe and NZZ), plus agencies in India (Adflow Digital), Dubai (Adflow Media), and Africa (adflow.africa). The GitHub org is taken. All four domains are taken (.com for sale on Afternic, .dev parked, .io is Adflow B.V., .ai is AdFlow Solutions). Three historical USPTO filings for "ADFLOW" exist (all dead/cancelled), but active companies may hold current filings. **Adform** (major European adtech DSP/DMP/SSP) has a product called "Adform FLOW" — direct phonetic collision. PyPI and npm are available but the brand space is unusable.

**mediator** — a real English word with an impossibly crowded namespace. "Mediator" returns millions of search results dominated by dispute resolution services, legal professionals, and the Gang of Four software design pattern. The term can never be owned even with aggressive marketing — it's like naming a database product "Database." While it accurately describes what the product does (ad mediation), the genericness works against it: `mediator` on PyPI is almost certainly contested, and no amount of SEO or marketing spend would push legal/dispute-resolution content out of the top search results. The creational.ai umbrella does not help here — "Creational.ai Mediator" would still drown in generic results, and the name provides zero signal that this is an adtech product.

**admedio** — the German ETL tax advisory network operates as "ADMEDIO" across **six cities** (Dresden, Magdeburg, Hamburg, Berlin, Dortmund, Krefeld) with active websites at admedio.com, admedio.net, and multiple city-specific domains. LinkedIn company pages exist. Even using admedio.dev would create brand confusion with an established multi-office brand.

**admux** — an advertising network called **ADMux** operates at admux.su offering banners, native ads, push notifications, and in-app ads. An adtech company called **Admuxer** (admuxer.com) provides programmatic solutions. And **Admixer** (admixer.com), a major global adtech vendor and IAB Tech Lab member, is phonetically confusable. Three adtech name collisions simultaneously.

**admesh** — a direct competitor in AI advertising. **AdMesh** launched in January 2026, joined the **NVIDIA Inception Program**, shipped a Python SDK (`admesh-python` on PyPI), and lists on Product Hunt. Separately, the `admesh` PyPI package has existed since the 1990s as an STL mesh processing library. The GitHub organization github.com/admesh is taken.

**adsync** — Microsoft's **ADSync** (Active Directory Synchronization) is an enterprise technology term with PowerShell cmdlets, Azure documentation, and millions of search results. Two separate companies also use the name. Every enterprise developer would assume this is an Active Directory tool.

**medibase** — five separate healthcare companies use this name across .com, .io, LinkedIn, and multiple countries, including a US company operating since **1993** that suffered a data breach (generating legal search results). The "medi" prefix reads universally as "medical."

**medops** — at least five active companies use "MedOps" (MedOps360, MedOps AI, MedOps Solutions, MedOP Solutions, Med Ops Staffing). The term universally reads as "medical operations." The .com, .pro, and .health TLDs are all taken by healthcare companies.

**tierform** — a company at tierform.com with LinkedIn presence. **mediateai** has its LinkedIn slug (linkedin.com/company/mediateai) claimed by an existing AR/computer vision company, and "mediate" is dominated by dispute resolution content. **medifi** is an active, funded ($1.78M) telehealth company with the domain, GitHub org, and app stores locked down.

## Three survivors ranked: admedi, tiersync, admedly

### #1 confirmed: admedi

**admedi** (6 characters) delivers the best balance across all seven criteria. The "ad" prefix instantly signals advertising; the "medi" root communicates mediation to anyone in adtech. It works as `import admedi`, as `admedi sync-tiers`, as a SaaS brand, and as a Cowork plugin that a non-technical monetization manager installs.

**Verified findings (2026-02-25):**

The software namespace is **clean**: no PyPI package, no npm package, no GitHub organization. A GitHub *user* named `admedi` exists (Eduardo Letelier, beginner developer with 11 trivial repos, 0 followers) — this is insignificant and does not block the `creational-ai/admedi` repo.

The trademark landscape is manageable but not pristine. A **Polish company** called AdMedi (admedi.pl) operates with **11-50 employees** (per ZoomInfo), originally building digital signage and self-service kiosks. The site may have pivoted — current content describes "effective marketing for medical industries." This is advertising-adjacent but in a different sub-sector, geography, and scale.

The larger proximity concern is **AdMedia** (admedia.com), a major US ad network headquartered in Woodland Hills, California. Verified: founded **1998**, **315 employees**, **30,076 LinkedIn followers**, claims 1 billion impressions/month and 60,000+ advertisers. The spelling difference (admedi vs. admedia) provides meaningful visual distinction in written contexts — the primary interaction mode for developer tools.

**Conflict the original research missed:** **admedi.net** is an active website for **Admedi Digital Marketing**, a small digital agency (~10 employees, ~$1M revenue) based in Bangladesh. This is a direct name overlap, though in a different product category (marketing agency vs. software platform) and at a scale unlikely to cause market confusion.

Domain status: **admedi.com** is parked (registered since 2005-08-23, Korean registrar Megazone Corp, expires 2026-08-23). **admedi.dev**, **admedi.io**, and **admedi.ai** all show NXDOMAIN — unregistered as of verification date. **This is moot** — all products and services will live under **creational.ai/admedi**, eliminating domain availability as a concern.

Scalability is strong. "admedi" is abstract enough to cover config management today and AI-powered intelligence tomorrow. It doesn't box the product into tiers, syncing, or any specific feature. The name carries the semantic weight of "mediation" without the length.

### #2 fallback: tiersync

**tiersync** (8 characters) has a very clean digital footprint. Google returns effectively **zero results** for the term as a product or brand. No company, no trademark, no PyPI package, no npm package, no GitHub org or repos (zero search results across all of GitHub).

**Verified findings (2026-02-25):**

**Correction from original research:** The original analysis claimed "no domain registration detected for any TLD." This was wrong. **tiersync.com has been registered since 2016-01-22** via Amazon Registrar, hosted on AWS Route 53 DNS, and was **renewed in December 2025** (expires 2027-01-22). The registrant uses Amazon's identity protection service. The domain does not serve HTTP content but is actively maintained by a technically sophisticated owner. **tiersync.io** and **tiersync.dev** do show NXDOMAIN — unregistered.

The critical weakness is **semantic clarity** — "Tier" signals tier management, and "sync" signals synchronization, but neither word communicates "ad mediation" to someone encountering the name cold. A non-technical ad ops person seeing "TierSync" in a plugin listing might not immediately understand what it does. Research into adtech vocabulary confirms this: the industry uses "mediation," "waterfall," "bidding," and "eCPM" — not "synchronization." "TierSync" reads more naturally as DevOps/infrastructure terminology.

Scalability is also a moderate concern. "Sync" fits Layer 1 (config sync) perfectly but feels constraining when the product evolves to Layer 2 (AI-powered intelligence) and Layer 3 (automated A/B testing). "Syncing" is not the core metaphor for AI recommendations or experiment orchestration.

CLI ergonomics are excellent: `tiersync configure`, `tiersync audit`. The Python import `import tiersync` is clean. As a SaaS brand, "TierSync" reads professional but skews DevOps/infrastructure rather than adtech.

### #3: admedly

**admedly** (7 characters) follows the modern SaaS naming pattern of Fastly, Bitly, and Assembly. The "ad" prefix signals advertising, and the "med" root embeds "mediation."

**Verified findings (2026-02-25):**

Domain availability confirmed: **all four TLDs** (.com, .io, .dev, .ai) are **unregistered** — verified via WHOIS and DNS. This is the cleanest domain picture of any candidate researched.

The software namespace is nearly clean. The GitHub repo **qzaidi/admedly** exists — confirmed as a single-commit repo from 2013 containing only a README with the title "admedly" and no source code (0 stars, 0 forks). The owner, Qasim Zaidi (VP Engineering at Tokopedia), also publishes an Amazon Alexa skill ("Nahajul Balagha — Sayings from Imam Ali") under the developer name "Admedly." Both are personal-project-level uses, not commercial entities.

The **deal-weakening problem** is SEO: searching "admedly" returns dictionary results for **"admittedly"** prominently (Merriam-Webster, Dictionary.com, Collins, Cambridge all surface). Google's spelling correction algorithms treat "admedly" as a likely misspelling of "admittedly." Every search interaction — users looking for docs, potential customers researching the product, developers seeking the CLI reference — would fight against dictionary autocorrect. Verbal communication also suffers; saying "admedly" in a meeting or podcast would be heard as "admittedly" by many listeners.

## Why the creational.ai umbrella doesn't change the ranking

A reasonable counter-argument: since all products live under creational.ai, names with existing market presence (like "adintel" or "mediator") could work because the parent brand provides differentiation. This argument is partially valid — the umbrella eliminates domain and SEO concerns — but it fails on three points that the umbrella cannot fix:

**1. Industry conversation confusion persists.** When you're in LevelPlay support channels, adtech Slack communities, or talking to ironSource reps, saying "our Ad Intel product" creates confusion with ironSource/Unity's own internal AdIntel division and Nielsen's Ad Intel product. This is day-to-day operational friction that no URL structure solves. Similarly, "our Mediator product" in an adtech conversation would be interpreted as a generic reference to mediation, not as a product name.

**2. Generic/descriptive terms can never be owned.** "Ad intel" is a category term (advertising intelligence) used by GroupM, VaynerMedia, and Ebiquity. "Mediator" is a common English word. The marketing effort required to carve out "Creational.ai Ad Intel" as distinct from Nielsen Ad Intel — in the exact same industry — would be far greater than building recognition for a name that's already yours. The creational.ai umbrella helps every name equally, so the relative ranking doesn't change.

**3. Trademark exposure is URL-independent.** Nielsen's trademark rights from their £26M AdIntel acquisition apply regardless of whether the product lives at adintel.com or creational.ai/adintel. If they see "Creational.ai Ad Intel" in the same industry, the legal exposure is the same.

**admedi** avoids all three problems: it's distinctive enough that nobody else is "admedi," descriptive enough to communicate "ad mediation," and abstract enough that no incumbent owns it as a category term.
